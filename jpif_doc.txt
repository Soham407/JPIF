Overview :
ACIC – Jnana Prabodhini Innovation Foundation (ACIC-JPIF) is a Section 8 company promoted by Jnana Prabodhini in collaboration with the Atal Innovation Mission (AIM), NITI Aayog, Government of India. The initiative aims to establish an Atal Community Incubation Centre (ACIC) focused on nurturing grassroots innovators, rural entrepreneurs, and community-led enterprises from underserved regions.
About JP
Jnana Prabodhini is a reputed social institution with over six decades of experience in rural development, entrepreneurship development, women empowerment, biotechnology, agriculture, and natural resource management across Maharashtra.
	•	About ACIC-JPIF
The proposed ACIC will serve as a catalyst for transforming innovative ideas into sustainable enterprises that create measurable social and economic impact.
Vision
• Promote grassroots innovation and entrepreneurship in underserved communities. • Support startups aligned with Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). • Enable rural innovators to build financially sustainable enterprises. • Create an inclusive innovation ecosystem connecting communities, institutions, mentors, and markets.
Objectives
• Identify and nurture innovators from rural and semi-urban areas. • Build entrepreneurship capabilities among youth, women, SHGs, farmers, FPOs, and community organizations. • Provide incubation, mentoring, technical, and business support to startups. • Facilitate market access, partnerships, and funding opportunities. • Develop scalable solutions in agriculture, biotechnology, renewable energy, sustainability, and health sectors.

Focus Sectors
• Agriculture & Allied Activities • Agri-Biotechnology • Renewable Energy & Environmental Sustainability • Health, Ayurveda & Nutraceuticals • Rural Livelihoods & Community Enterprises
Community-Centric Approach
The incubation model is designed around a bottom-up community entrepreneurship approach.  Key Target Groups: • Self Help Groups (SHGs) • Farmers and Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) • Rural Youth • Students & Researchers • Entrepreneurs • Voluntary Organizations • Ayurvedic Practitioners  Key interventions include design thinking workshops, ideathons, capacity-building programs, entrepreneurship mentoring, outreach activities, and market linkage support.

Physical Incubation
Transforming Ideas into Sustainable Enterprises
The Physical Incubation Program is designed for innovators, startups, community entrepreneurs, SHGs, FPOs, rural youth, researchers, and enterprises requiring intensive handholding and access to physical infrastructure.
The program focuses on nurturing innovations from the idea stage to commercialization through structured mentoring, technology support, market linkages, and business development assistance.
The incubation duration typically ranges from 10 to 12 months, depending on the maturity and requirements of the startup.
Incubation Levels
Level 1: Idea to Proof of Concept (PoC)
This stage supports innovators in validating their ideas and developing initial prototypes.
Support Offered
	•	Design Thinking Workshops 
	•	Problem Identification & Validation 
	•	Product Concept Development 
	•	Access to Domain Experts 
	•	Access to Makerspace Facilities 
	•	Prototype Development Support 
	•	Market Need Assessment 
	•	Initial Business Model Development 
Expected Outcomes
	•	Validated problem statement 
	•	Working prototype or Proof of Concept 
	•	Initial customer feedback 
	•	Defined value proposition

Selection Process
Transparent and Merit-Based Evaluation
The selection process is designed to identify innovators with the highest potential for impact and scalability.
Stage 1: Application Screening
Evaluation Criteria:
	•	Problem Relevance 
	•	Innovation Potential 
	•	Community Impact 
	•	Feasibility 
Stage 2: Preliminary Review
Applications are reviewed by the ACIC team and domain experts.
Stage 3: Presentation / Pitch
Applicants present:
	•	Problem Statement 
	•	Proposed Solution 
	•	Business Opportunity 
	•	Expected Impact 
Stage 4: Expert Evaluation
Assessment by:
	•	Sector Experts 
	•	Industry Mentors 
	•	Business Advisors 
	•	Incubation Team 
Stage 5: Due Diligence
Verification of:
	•	Team Capability 
	•	Technical Feasibility 
	•	Market Opportunity 
	•	Scalability 
Stage 6: Final Selection
Selected applicants receive:
	•	Offer Letter 
	•	Incubation Agreement 
	•	Onboarding Support
